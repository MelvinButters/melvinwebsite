Fizzlebang’s Tactical Notes on Reckless Assault

Applied Violence Theory – Barbarian Edition

Auta; this technique assumes you have control over your emotional sensors. EXTREMELY IMPORTANT!

I. Philosophy of Recklessness

To attack recklessly is not to attack foolishly — though the distinction is subtle and often ignored. Recklessness is the conscious decision to abandon caution in favor of overwhelming intent.

You are not defending yourself because you are declaring, quite clearly, that the enemy will not survive long enough for their counterattack to matter.

II. Mental Preparation (Very Important, Actually)

  • Empty your mind of doubt, fear, and tactical restraint.
  • Focus exclusively on the target and the satisfaction of impact.
  • Ensure Toodles is not jumping up and down around you. This is cute and distracting.

Hesitation negates the entire technique. If you are thinking about defense, you are doing it wrong.

III. Execution Guidelines

1. Commit Fully

When you strike, do not pull back, feint, or guard. Open your stance. Overextend. Let momentum carry the blow through the target and into whatever unfortunate object exists behind them.

2. Ignore the Opening

Yes, you are exposed. Yes, they can hit you more easily now. This is acceptable. You are trading safety for certainty.

3. Press the Advantage

Once the first reckless strike lands, continue the assault without pause. Momentum is your shield now. Doubt is the enemy.

IV. Common Mistakes

  • Thinking only of the offensive: A strong attack can sometimes be your best defence.
  • Practicing on my furniture: As attacking recklessly increases your chances of a powerful strike by 5%, please do not smite my couch again.
  • Apologizing mid-swing: It will not have any negative affects Auta, it's just not socially acceptable.

V. When NOT to Attack Recklessly

Rare, but worth noting:

  • If you find yourself surrounded by many strong enemies.
  • If you're better off spending your time casting a spell, or moving further to an objective.
  • Again, when it is my furniture or belongings.
Final reminder: Reckless assault is not about anger alone. It is about trust — trust in your strength, your endurance, and your friends to finish what you begin.

For Auta

If you are reading this, then the moment has already passed — or is closer than either of us would like to admit. I have never been good at saying the important things aloud, so I leave them here instead, where the words cannot tremble or be interrupted.

The world will always demand pieces of you. Time, courage, certainty. Sometimes it will ask for more than is fair. When that happens, remember this: the things you hold dear are not weaknesses. They are the reason you endure at all.

Protect them. Protect the people, the ideals, the quiet hopes you carry when no one is watching. Not because the world deserves them — but because you do.

Say what must be said while breath and chance still allow it. Regret is a heavier burden than fear, and silence has ended more stories than any blade ever has.

Whatever comes next, know that I believed in you — not because you were flawless, but because you chose to care in a world that makes that choice difficult.

— Fizzlebang

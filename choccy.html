Kenku Hot Chocolate

A warm, mimic-friendly brew recorded in the Old Recipe Tome.

Introduction

Little kenkus are easily startled creatures, their feathers puffing at the slightest creak or shadow. A warm cup of cocoa is an old roost tradition — a gentle comfort that calms trembling wings and softens mimic-echoed worries. This recipe is passed from perch to perch, brewed to soothe frightened hearts and remind young kenkus that even in a loud and unpredictable world, sweetness can be summoned with a careful stir.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup milk (or oat milk)
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Pinch of cinnamon
  • Tiny pinch of salt
  • Whipped cream (optional)
  • Toasted seeds or crushed nuts
Method
  • Warm the milk until it gently steams — "bubble-bubble" as Kenku say.
  • Mix cocoa, honey, cinnamon and salt into a smooth paste.
  • Whisk the hot milk into the paste until silky.
  • Return briefly to heat for deeper warmth.
  • Top with cream and toasted seeds. Tap cup twice — "tak‑tak!"

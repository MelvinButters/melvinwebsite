Fizzlebang’s Radiant Punch Notes — Apprentice Edition

Instructions left behind for your training

Recipient: Apprentice, take care with your strikes

If your fist starts glowing too early, stop immediately and unclench. We don’t want any fires before the proper moment.

I. Why These Notes Exist

These notes are here to guide you in safely channeling radiant force through a punch. Follow them carefully to keep your fists intact and your energy flowing correctly.

II. Preparing Yourself

  • Sacred Focus: Align your inner energy carefully. Too much excitement? Dial it back. Too little? Let it grow gently.
  • Intent: Pick a simple phrase in your heart and repeat it silently to guide the energy.
  • Stance: Feet at 37° outward. Steady yourself, like a tree rooted in goodness.

III. Components You’ll Need

1. Striking Surface

Gauntlet or bare fist, clean and dry. Sticky hands may redirect energy away from your fist.

2. Radiant Token

A small token — sunburst, feather, or glowing rock. Keep it nearby; it helps energy stay on track.

IV. Understanding Punch Power

Think of your Holy Punch Power like this:

Holy Punch Power ≈ Divine Energy × Punch Angle ÷ Stability Factor
  • Divine Energy = How focused your fist is
  • Punch Angle = The smooth path your fist travels
  • Stability Factor = Your calmness and control over distractions

If your punch fizzles or glows oddly, breathe, adjust, and try again.

V. Punch Execution Sequence

  1. Draw energy into your fist with a light clench and deep inhale.
  2. Channel faith along your arm like a warm current. Fizzy = good; crunchy = stop.
  3. Wind up slowly, one holy syllable per heartbeat. Suggested mantra: "Lux… Solis…".
  4. Release with exhalation, ensuring radiant energy exits just before impact.
  5. Aim carefully; energy will follow your focus.

Troubleshooting

  • Glow is wrong: Green = nature interference; Purple = accidental necromancy; Red = too excited. Adjust carefully.
  • Fist feels light: Overcharged. Touch the ground. Prefer dirt.
  • Target explodes more than expected: Note it for next time. Your precision is improving.

End of Apprentice Notes — Revision IV

If something catches fire, that’s okay. If something sentient does, fill out Form 12-B carefully.
